Accessibility Statement

Conexion Insurance Agency is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all people.

Effective Date: January 1, 2025

Our Commitment

Conexion Insurance Agency is committed to ensuring that our website is accessible to people with disabilities. We strive to meet or exceed the requirements of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA, as well as applicable federal and state accessibility laws.

Measures We Take

To support accessibility, Conexion Insurance Agency takes the following measures:

  • Include accessibility as a requirement in our website design and development process.
  • Provide clear navigation and consistent page structure throughout the site.
  • Use sufficient color contrast for all text and meaningful visuals.
  • Ensure all images include descriptive alternative text.
  • Make all functionality available via keyboard navigation.
  • Use semantic HTML to support screen readers and assistive technologies.
  • Provide text alternatives for non-text content.

Conformance Status

We are actively working toward conformance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. While we strive to adhere to accepted guidelines and standards, it may not always be possible to do so in all areas of the website. We continue to seek out solutions that will improve accessibility across our entire site.

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some content on this website may not yet fully meet all WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines. We are continually working to address any gaps. Third-party content, such as embedded maps and social media feeds, may not fully conform to accessibility standards as those are outside our direct control.

Alternative Access

If you are unable to access any content on this website due to a disability, please contact us by phone or email and we will provide the information you need in an accessible format, or assist you in completing any transaction or activity on the site.

Formal Complaints

We are committed to resolving any accessibility complaints promptly. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the relevant federal or state agency responsible for enforcing applicable accessibility laws.
